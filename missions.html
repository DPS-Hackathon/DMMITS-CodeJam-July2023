Missions
Since its founding, ISRO has launched 124 spacecraft missions and 92 launch missions
These are some of the most important and influential missions
Chandrayaan

Chandrayaan I

Launch: 22 Oct 2008

Launcher: PSLV-XL

Chandrayaan-1 was India's first lunar probe, this mission was a major boost to India's space program, as India researched and developed its own technology in order to explore the Moon

Chandrayaan II

Launch: 22 Jul 2019

Launcher: LVM-III M1

Chandrayaan-2 was launched from Satish Dhawan Space Centre. It consists of a lunar orbiter, lander and rover, all developed in India. It's main scientific objective was to map the location and abundance of lunar water.

Chandrayaan III

Launch: 14 Jul 2023

Launcher: LVM-III M4

Chandrayaan-3 is third and most recent lunar Indian Space Research exploration mission under Chandrayaan programme. It consists of a lander named Vikram and a rover named Pragyan. Chandrayaan-3 mission is a stepping stone towards ISROâ€™s future interplanetary missions

Mangalyaan

Mangalyaan (MOM)

Launch: 5 Nov 2012

Launcher: PSLV-XL

Mars Orbiter Mission (MOM) was India's first interplanetary mission to planet Mars. ISRO became the fourth space agency to successfully send a spacecraft to Mars orbit. Though the mission life was planned to be 6 months, MOM completed 7 years in mars's orbit in 2021

Upcoming Missions

Aditya-L1

Status: Approved

Launcher: PSLV-XL

Aditya-L1 is the first Indian mission to study the solar corona (outermost part of sun's atmostphere). The mission is scheduled to be launched on 26 August 2023. This will provide a greater advantage of observing the solar activities and its effect on space weather in real time

X-PoSat

Status: Planned

Launcher: SSLV

The X-ray Polarimeter Satellite (XPoSat) is a planned space observatory to study polarization of cosmic X-rays. It is planned to be launched in 2023. XPoSat will study the 50 brightest known sources in the universe, including pulsars, black holes, active galactic nuclei, supernova remnants etc.

Gaganyaan 1

Status: Planned

Launcher: LVM 3

Gaganyaan Orbital Vehicle is an Indian crewed orbital spacecraft intended to be the basis of the Indian Human Spaceflight Programme. The spacecraft is being designed to carry three people. This will be the first of two flight tests prior to the real crewed mission.

NISAR

Status: Approved

Launcher: GSLV II

The NASA-ISRO Synthetic Aperture Radar (NISAR) satellite, will use advanced radar imaging to map the elevation of Earth's land and ice masses 4 to 6 times a month at resolutions of 5 to 10 meters. It is designed to observe and measure some of the planet's most complex natural processes

Shukrayaan

Status: Approved

Launcher: GSLV III

Shukrayaan is a planned orbiter to study the structure, composition and dynamics of the atmosphere of Venus. If successfull, this would be the 3rd planet that ISRO has its orbiters on, after Mars and Moon

Gaganyaan 3

Status: Planned

Launcher: LVM 3

Gaganyaan 3 would be India's first crewed misison to space. If successful, India would become the fourth country in the world (after USA, Soviet Union and China) to independently send humans in space.
