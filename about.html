About

The Indian Space Research Organisation, commonly known as ISRO, is the national space agency of India. The organisation is involved in science, engineering and technology to harvest the benefits of outer space for India and the mankind. It operates under the Department of Space (DOS), the Chairman of ISRO also acts as the executive of DOS, which is overseen by the Prime Minister. The activities of ISRO are guided by its Chairman, who would also be the secretary of DOS and Chairman of Space commission, the apex body that formulates the policies and overseas the implementation of the Indian Space Programme.

Founding

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) was founded on August 15, 1969, with the vision of harnessing space technology for national development and various peaceful purposes. Its establishment was a result of the collective efforts and vision of several key individuals, including Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, Dr. Homi Jehangir Bhabha, Dr. P. Satish Dhawan and last but not the least Dr. Vikram Sarabhai, who is also often regarded as the father of the Indian space program.

ISRO's achievements and contributions have garnered international recognition, and it remains a symbol of India's advancements in space technology and research.

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) was founded on August 15, 1969, with the vision of harnessing space technology for national development and various peaceful purposes. Its establishment was a result of the collective efforts and vision of several key individuals, including Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, Dr. Homi Jehangir Bhabha, Dr. P. Satish Dhawan and last but not the least Dr. Vikram Sarabhai, who is also often regarded as the father of the Indian space program.

ISRO's achievements and contributions have garnered international recognition, and it remains a symbol of India's advancements in space technology and research.

Dr. Vikram Sarabhai, an eminent space scientist and visionary, played a pivotal role in the establishment of ISRO. He recognized the importance of space technology for India's development and advocated its peaceful use.

Before the formation of ISRO, the Indian National Committee for Space Research (INCOSPAR) was established in 1962 to conduct space-related activities in India. As pointed earlier, Dr. Sarabhai was the driving force behind INCOSPAR's formation.

On August 15, 1969, INCOSPAR was restructured and renamed as ISRO. ISRO was thus established in 1969 and was officially constituted as an autonomous government agency under the Department of Space

The primary objective of ISRO is to develop space technology and its application for various national tasks, including tasks like communication, meteorology, resource management and many more.

In its initial years, ISRO focused on developing satellites, beginning with the successful launch of the first Indian satellite Aryabhata, using a Soviet launch vehicle in 1975.
